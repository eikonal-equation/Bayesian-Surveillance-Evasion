A Grid Refinement Study of Alg-PC

We conduct a grid refinement study for the algorithm using piecewise constant model on the second example in paper. $\mathcal{G}$'s are chosen as $N\times N$ uniform square decompositions, with $N = 10, 20, 40$. We use $Q_*$ instead of the learnable optimality $\bar{Q}_*$ (see here) to compute the regret metrics of performance.


As we can see from the middle figure, with $T = 60,000$ episodes, Alg-PC yields a lower averaged regret on a 10$\times$10 $\mathcal{G}$ than on a 20$\times$20 grid for the first 40,000 episodes, while the averaged regret on a 40$\times$40 grid remains much higher until the very end. The right figure consolidates this through another regret metric, the experimentally observed capture probability.

In the left figure for $N=20$, we observe that the (non-averaged) differences between $Q_i$ and $Q_*$ are pushed down to a much lower level, compared with $N = 10$. This indicates that with $N=20$ Alg-PC is able to explore paths which are closer to become optimal. $N = 40$ has the same tendency, however, more episodes are needed for the advantage of using this finer grid to become observable.
